#34 Test Others' Work

This is very hands-on and particularly simple to do, but I think it is really underutilized. There is such a vast amount of high-fidelity work out there that can be tested at any time and pretty much everyone has access to it. With high-fidelity work I talk about web and mobile apps on the Apple or Google app store (especially those from your competitors).

Use those apps and test them (for your benefit).

We did this for one of our clients a couple of weeks ago. Using others’ work is an amazing way to learn and find out whether what you are envisioning for your app may or may not work. One huge benefit is that it saves you loads of time on the actual preparation of wireframes or prototypes, but it also makes planning much easier: no deadlines for example to get prototypes to the exact state of fidelity that you may need, you won't run into issues where screens aren't fully prepared or not working as planned.

  • How-To or What to consider
  • • Think about what the actual goal(s) of the user is or would be in your application
  • • Find flows within at least 2-3 live apps that get close to the goal(s)
  • • Do not worry about the visual design, apps' branding or similar
  • • During testing, make sure to randomize the lineup so that testers are not biased on what they already used before (more on this in a later research hack)

If you wonder how to best set up such sessions or what are the key ingredients, please reach out to me.
